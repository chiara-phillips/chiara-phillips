Growing up, both of my parents were permaculture design instructors who incorporated their teachings right in our backyard. As a kid, I would tend to our family’s chickens before school and turn compost after school. Our backyard garden was full of opportunities for experiential learning and problem-solving and deeply informed the person I am today- that is- someone who is incredibly passionate about food security, regenerative agriculture, and meeting environmental challenges with nature-based solutions. 🌱

Today, I bring this unique perspective into my geospatial career. My mission is to infuse my expertise in remote sensing, machine learning, and geospatial analysis with my superpower for cartographic storytelling to provide actionable insights that can help create a more regenerative and equitable world for generations to come. 🌎✨

Some of my personal highlights include:

✅ Generating a 10-crop spectral signature library using JavaScript in Google Earth Engine to streamline and enhance the accuracy of USDA crop classification processes 

✅ Co-developing a Google Earth Engine web application that allows users to monitor global agricultural conditions on-the-fly 

✅ Project managing multiple NASA DEVELOP teams in employing remote sensing and machine learning for environmental research projects for stakeholders around the world 

✅ Piloting a composting program within UCLA's graduate housing complexes and securing funding for program expansion 

Outside of work, you'll find me gardening in my backyard 👩🏻‍🌾, baking pastries from scratch 🧁, or rock climbing at a local crag! 🧗🏻‍♀️
